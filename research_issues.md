# Research Questions for Margaret Alvord Biography

## Issue 1: Peterson Publishing, Chicago (c. 1928-1932)
What was the nature of this publishing house? Was it common for women of this era to have roles on its editorial staff?

## Issue 2: League of Women Voters in Evanston (c. 1930s-40s)
What were the primary activities and political focuses of this chapter during this period? What does this tell us about Margaret's civic engagement?

## Issue 3: The 'Marriage Bar' for Women Teachers
Margaret left teaching after three years, prior to her marriage. Was this a personal choice or a result of institutional policies, often known as 'marriage bars', that forced female teachers to resign upon marriage?
